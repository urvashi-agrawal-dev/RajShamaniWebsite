The Story Behind the Questions

From a curious kid in Indore to conversations that matter on a global stage.

Early Days in Indore

Growing up in Indore, I was that kid who asked too many questions. While others were content with answers, I was fascinated by the 'why' behind everything. This curiosity wasn't always appreciated in traditional classrooms, but it shaped who I am today.

My parents often joke that I interviewed them more than they interviewed my teachers during parent-teacher meetings. Little did we know this would become my superpower.

The Entrepreneurial Spark

My entrepreneurial journey began not with a grand plan, but with a simple realization: the best businesses solve real problems. I started young, failed often, and learned that every failure was just expensive education.

What fascinated me wasn't just building businesses, but understanding the minds behind successful ventures. This led me to seek out conversations with entrepreneurs, which eventually evolved into something much bigger.

Why Podcasting?

Podcasting chose me, not the other way around. I realized that the most valuable conversations I was having were happening off-camera, in green rooms, over coffee. These unguarded moments revealed the real stories behind success.

"Figuring Out with Raj Shamani" became my way of bringing these intimate conversations to the world. It's not about having all the answers—it's about asking the right questions and being genuinely curious about the human experience.

Personal Philosophy

"Some people meditate for peace. I ask questions."

This isn't just a catchy line—it's how I live. Every conversation is an opportunity to understand something new about human nature, success, failure, and everything in between. I believe that the right question at the right time can change someone's entire perspective.

Whether I'm talking to a tech billionaire or a street vendor, the curiosity remains the same. Everyone has a story worth telling, and everyone has something to teach us.

Life Beyond the Mic

When I'm not recording podcasts or building businesses, you'll find me with Crypto—my dog, not the currency. He's probably the only one who doesn't care about my questions and just wants his walks on time.

I believe in living the questions I ask. Whether it's about health, relationships, or personal growth, I'm constantly experimenting and learning. After all, how can you ask authentic questions about life if you're not actively living it?

What Drives Me

At the core, I'm driven by the belief that everyone is figuring it out as they go. The most successful people aren't those with all the answers—they're the ones who ask better questions and aren't afraid to admit when they don't know something.

My goal is simple: to create conversations that matter, ask questions that provoke thought, and help people realize that it's okay not to have everything figured out. We're all just figuring it out, one conversation at a time.
